My name is Melanie Greene. I attend Pace University in New York City, yes the city that never sleeps. The capital of the world. I am a junior studying information technology with a concentration in web design and digital media. I also have a minor in marketing.

I am belong to the Seidenberg School of Computer Science and Information Systems . My home away from home. #SeidenbergPride

I have taken so many great classes. I have learned how to use SQL and create a database in my Database Management course, explored Java in my Introduction to Programming I course, experienced HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in Web Design and Digital media, among many others.

So, how did I choose my major?

Entering college I was so undecided. I had no idea what I wanted to pursue. Where did I see myself? It was rough. In my Fall 2014 semester I took the following classes:

And..... I discovered something. Something I never knew I could do. I liked learning how to code. I felt like I was discovering me. It was eye opening.

My Web Design for Non-Profits course changed my life. I worked with an individual who had a disability (Down Syndrome) to create a project based on his interests and abilities. We made a video game using an app on the iPad called GamePress and documented our time together by creating an iMovie.

This experience blossomed my passion for giving back to others. I have worked as a mentor for two different individuals on the Autism Spectrum where I identified educational technologies tailored to address the respective communication challenges of two students. I utilized games to expand vocabulary and improve word usage for both students.

Over this past summer I worked on a research project with my professor. We researched wearable devices that would enable individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities to proactively manage their health care choices.

I discovered a passion for technology.
